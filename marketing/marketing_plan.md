# The Sovereign Heritage Marketing Plan

## 1. Objective: The "Inheritocracy" & DFL Market
Our target demographic is the "Inheritocracy" and the "DFL" (Down-from-London) Senior Leadership Teams. We are selling an exclusive, high-stakes crucible, positioning the Bembridge Fort not as a museum, but as an operational arena.

## 2. The Cassie Multiplier (Revenue Logic)
We must shift the pricing paradigm completely. The traditional heritage model asks for a £10 "tour fee". We are positioning "The Traitor Days" at a **£150,000 price point** per corporate event.
This is not a tour; it is a **Leadership Relationship Accelerator**. For an SLT, the cost of siloed thinking and miscommunication dwarfs our fee. We are selling them the "Refusal Wall" experience—a high-stakes environment where their operational vulnerabilities are exposed and repaired in real-time.

## 3. The Eleanor Hook (Narrative Brand)
Our primary narrative brand is **"The Enigma of the Down."** 
We are not selling a day out at the seaside. We are selling a descent into a latency-free decision-making environment. The story of Master Raymond Edmunds and the XDO computing pioneers provides the emotional and intellectual stakes necessary to ground the corporate participants in a shared, profound heritage.

## 4. Aesthetic Directive: Explorecore Minimalism
All pitch documents and operational materials must utilise the **Explorecore sub-trend**. This means calm editorial minimalism for the preliminary pitch documents—clean layouts, generous negative space, and modern typography—which sharply contrasts with the high-stakes, "Brittle Realism" and "Boardroom" tension they will experience upon arrival at the 1867 Bar.
