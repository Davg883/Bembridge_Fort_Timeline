# Sovereign Heritage Aesthetic Enforcement: "Vibe Coding" Check

*This directive outlines the non-negotiable visual and thematic standards for all Bembridge Fort Trust materials.*

## 1. "Brittle Realism" (1940s Operative Tone)
- **Texture:** Use 35mm grain. Never use digital smoothing. The imagery must feel tactile and historically grounded.
- **Lighting:** Employ Rembrandt-style lighting (high-contrast chiaroscuro). Shadows are as important as the subjects.
- **Surface:** Represent cold stone, greasepaint, and dust. Reject "Hollywood-style" clean aesthetics.
- **Keywords:** Utilitarian, Tactical, Atmospheric.
- **Tokens:** `[GRAIN_CHECK_1950]`, `[INK_JITTER_1880]`.

## 2. "Notes App Chic" (Archival Aesthetic)
- **Materiality:** Use tea-stained, aged paper textures with realistic imperfections (e.g., coffee rings, smeared ink). Never use clean white backgrounds.
- **Typography:** Hand-written marginalised notes alongside brutalist, functional typefaces like Transport Font or Gill Sans.
- **Composition:** Asymmetric, chaotic but calculated. Documents should appear "snatched from a secure archive."
- **Language:** Strictly en-GB orthography (Centre, Theatre, Grey). Use period-accurate British terms (Lorry, Pavement).

## 3. "Explorecore Minimalism" (2026 Pitch Tone)
- **Aura:** Discreet, high-status, "Pukka."
- **Use Case:** SLT pitch decks, UHNWI invitations, and financial modelling.
- **Palette:** Calm editorial minimalism, generous negative space.
- **Contrast:** The clean, rational layout of the pitch documents must violently contrast with the high-stakes, brittle reality the executives will step into at the Fort.
- **Tokens:** `[WAX_DEPTH_2026]`, `[MACRO_FIDELITY_4K]`.
