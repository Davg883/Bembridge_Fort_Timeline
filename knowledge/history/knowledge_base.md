# Bembridge Fort: Core Knowledge Base

## The Palmerston Folly
Bembridge Fort originated as a Victorian stronghold, commissioned in 1862 and constructed for £48,925. This hexagonal "Barrack Keep", built with split flint and red brick walls, was defended by the "Green Gunners" (the Isle of Wight Rifles, Princess Beatrice's Own), who were distinguished by their dark green uniforms with black facings and Carisbrooke Castle badges.

## The Electronic Shield
During WWII, the Fort became a critical node in the Solent's defence. It housed the AMES Type 41 (CHEL) centimetric radar, a revolutionary 10cm "pencil beam" technology. Inside the XDO (Extended Defence Officer) Room, personnel monitored the Underwater Indicator Loops—magnetic cables tracking Nazi U-boats. This system fed into the "Bembridge Reflex", a sensory-fusion network reducing the response latency of the heavy 9.2-inch guns at Culver Battery to just 45-90 seconds.

## Greasepaint & Sausages
Amidst the military operations lived the human heart of the Fort: Master Raymond Edmunds, a 10-year-old boy. As the youngest ENSA performer of WWII, he often sang "The Good Ship Lollipop" on makeshift stages of carpet-covered packing cases, entertaining over 1,000 sailors. A poignant artifact of his time was his nightly habit: sneaking a handful of sausages from the Officers' Mess into his pockets for his father's breakfast, all whilst wearing his performance greasepaint to his school desk the next morning.
